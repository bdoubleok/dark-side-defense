## What's New

Every coverage play now includes **expandable DL alignment details** that adapt based on your hash filter settings with **directional context** (LEFT/RIGHT) and **formation-specific fronts**.

## 📚 DL Technique Reference

### **Interior Techniques (Tackles):**

| Tech | Alignment | Gap Control | When to Use |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **0-tech** | Nose on center | Both A-gaps | 3-3 fronts, goal line |
| **1-tech** | Inside shade of guard | A-gap (one side) | Away from strength, shade technique |
| **2i-tech** | Inside eye of guard | Penetrate A-gap | Pass rush, aggressive |
| **3-tech** | Outside shoulder of guard | B-gap | Pass rush, TO STRENGTH |
| **4i-tech** | Inside shade of tackle | Inside B-gap/C-gap | 3-3 fronts, two-gap |

### **Edge Techniques (Ends):**

| Tech | Alignment | Gap Control | When to Use |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **5-tech** | Outside shoulder of tackle | C-gap | Base DE alignment, two-gap |
| **6-tech** | Head-up on tackle | C-gap (two-gap) | vs run, power |
| **7-tech** | Inside shoulder of TE | Squeeze C-gap | TE attached, force run |
| **9-tech** | Outside shoulder of TE | Wide, contain | Pass rush, wide 9 |

## 🧠 Macdonald's Front Principles

### **"Over" Front:**
```
STRENGTH →  [DE-3T-1T-DE]  ← AWAY

• 3T to strength (trips/TE side)
• 1T away from strength
• Used when strength is clear
```

**When:**
- Trips to field (wide side)
- TE attached to field
- Formation has clear strength to wide side

**Why:**
- 3T to strength = pass rush toward threats
- 1T weak = gap control away
- Slants toward strength

**Directional Example (Left Hash, Trips Right):**
```
Trips RIGHT = Field (more space)

🔧 NICKEL 2-4 OVER

▸ 3T to RIGHT (trips side, field)
▸ 1T to LEFT (boundary)
▸ DE: 5-tech both sides
```

---

### **"Under" Front:**
```
STRENGTH →  [DE-1T-3T-DE]  ← AWAY

• 3T away from strength
• 1T to strength
• Reverse of "Over"
```

**When:**
- Strength to boundary (short side)
- Want to slant away from strength
- TE to boundary, trips to boundary

**Why:**
- Forces runs into strength side
- Slants away for cutback pursuit
- Gap integrity on compressed side

**Directional Example (Left Hash, TE Left to Boundary):**
```
TE LEFT = Boundary (short side)

🔧 NICKEL 2-4 UNDER

▸ 3T to LEFT (TE side, boundary)
▸ 1T to RIGHT (field)
▸ DE: 7-tech on TE (LEFT, squeeze), 5-tech away (RIGHT)
```

---

### **"Balanced" Front:**
```
FIELD →  [DE-3T-1T-DE]  ← BOUNDARY

• 3T to field (wide side)
• 1T to boundary (short side)
• No clear offensive strength
```

**When:**
- 2x2 spread (balanced)
- Empty formations
- Middle hash
- No TE attached

**Why:**
- Field side = more space, need 3T
- Boundary = less space, 1T suffices
- Symmetrical approach

**Directional Example (Left Hash, 2x2 Spread):**
```
Left Hash = Field RIGHT, Boundary LEFT

🔧 NICKEL 2-4 BALANCED

▸ 3T to RIGHT (field)
▸ 1T to LEFT (boundary)
▸ DE: 5-tech both sides
```

## 🔧 **CRITICAL: Nickel 3-3 Mint Alignment**

### **3-3 Front = 3 Down Linemen (NO Traditional DEs)**

**Correct Alignment:**
```
🔧 NICKEL 3-3 MINT

▸ 4i-tech both sides (inside shade of tackles)
▸ 0-tech nose (head-up on center)
▸ Edge Rushers wide (OLB/DE hybrid, 9-tech or contain)
```

**Visual:**
```
        Edge            0-tech           Edge
       (OLB)            (Nose)           (OLB)
         ↓                ↓                ↓
    9-tech            Center           9-tech
         ↓                                 ↓
      4i-tech    [Guard-Guard]         4i-tech
    (inside        (protected)        (inside
     shade                              shade
    tackle)                            tackle)
```

**Key Points:**
- ❌ **NO 3-tech or 1-tech in 3-3 fronts**
- ❌ **NO traditional defensive ends**
- ✅ **4i-tech = inside shade of tackles** (both sides)
- ✅ **0-tech = nose** (head-up on center)
- ✅ **Edge Rushers = OLB/DE hybrids** (stand-up or hand-down)
- ✅ **9-tech or contain** (wide rush lanes)

**Why This Matters:**
- 3-3 = **3-man defensive line** + 3 linebackers
- Different gap responsibilities than 4-man fronts
- Edge players are **hybrid LB/DE**, not pure DEs
- Creates **8-man box** naturally

## 🎯 Formation-Specific Examples

### **Example 1: Trips to Field (Left Hash)**

**Hash Filter Settings:**
- Hash: Left
- Formation: 3x1 Trips  
- Strength: To Field (right side = wide side)

**Cover 6 Alignment:**
```
🔧 NICKEL 2-4 OVER

▸ 3T to RIGHT (trips side, field) - CRITICAL
▸ 1T to LEFT (boundary, shade nose)
▸ DE: 5-tech both sides
```

**Why This Alignment:**
- **3T to RIGHT:** Pass rush toward maximum threat (3 receivers + more space)
- **1T to LEFT:** Gap control on short side, shade nose toward field
- **5-tech both sides:** Base alignment, contain both edges
- **RIGHT = field = trips side = where the action is**

**What This Means:**
- Your 3-tech tackle goes to the RIGHT
- Your 1-tech tackle goes to the LEFT  
- Both DEs stay in base 5-tech
- Front called: "Over Right"

---

### **Example 2: TE Attached to Boundary (Right Hash)**

**Hash Filter Settings:**
- Hash: Right
- Formation: 2x2 TE Attached
- Strength: To Boundary (right side = short side)

**Cover 3 Sky Alignment:**
```
🔧 NICKEL 2-4 UNDER

▸ 3T to RIGHT (TE side, boundary)
▸ 1T to LEFT (field)
▸ DE: 7-tech on TE (RIGHT, squeeze), 5-tech away (LEFT)
```

**Why This Alignment:**
- **3T to RIGHT:** Control B-gap next to TE, force run into short side
- **1T to LEFT:** Gap control on wide side
- **7-tech on TE (RIGHT):** Squeeze TE, take away C-gap run
- **5-tech away (LEFT):** Base alignment away from TE
- **RIGHT = boundary = TE side = where we want to suffocate the run**

**What This Means:**
- Your 3-tech tackle goes to RIGHT (boundary/TE)
- Your 1-tech tackle goes to LEFT (field)
- DE on TE side (RIGHT) plays 7-tech squeeze
- DE away from TE (LEFT) plays 5-tech base
- Front called: "Under Right"

---

### **Example 3: 2x2 Spread (Middle Hash)**

**Hash Filter Settings:**
- Hash: Middle
- Formation: 2x2 Spread
- Strength: None (balanced)

**Cover 4 Quarters Alignment:**
```
🔧 NICKEL 2-4 BALANCED

▸ 3T to field
▸ 1T to boundary
▸ DE: 5-tech both sides
```

**Why This Alignment:**
- **Middle hash:** Equal space both sides, but call one side "field"
- **3T to field:** Wide side gets 3-tech by default
- **Balanced:** Symmetrical, no slant needed
- **No LEFT/RIGHT:** Generic because middle hash is ambiguous

**What This Means:**
- Determine which is field based on slight advantage
- Typically assign field to wider side or offense tendency
- DEs stay base 5-tech, no special technique needed
- Front called: "Balanced"

---

### **Example 4: Empty (Any Hash)**

**Hash Filter Settings:**
- Hash: Left (example)
- Formation: Empty
- Strength: None

**Cover 1 w/ Spy Alignment:**
```
🔧 3-3-5 OVER FLEX

▸ 3T to RIGHT (field)
▸ 1T to LEFT (boundary)
▸ DE: 5-tech both (contain rush)
```

**Why This Alignment:**
- **Empty = pass:** Speed rush emphasis, but must CONTAIN
- **3T to RIGHT (field):** Rush toward wider side
- **Contain rush:** Keep QB in pocket, don't let him escape edges
- **Different personnel:** 3-3-5 = only 3 LBs, 5 DBs

**What This Means:**
- Same as balanced front (3T field, 1T boundary)
- DEs play 5-tech but emphasis is CONTAIN (not just sack)
- Stop QB scramble, force throw from pocket
- Front called: "3-3-5 Over"

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: What does LEFT/RIGHT mean in the alignment?**
A: Actual field direction based on hash position. Left hash + trips right = trips are to the RIGHT (field side).

**Q: Why does alignment say "Under" when I expected "Over"?**
A: Formation strength to boundary = Under front. Strength to field = Over front.

**Q: Do I have to expand alignment every play?**
A: No! Hash filter result box shows front summary. Expand for full detail when learning.

**Q: What if I don't know techniques?**
A: Just read the text with context. "3T to RIGHT (trips side, field)" tells you everything.

**Q: Does alignment change based on hash filter?**
A: YES! Same coverage shows different LEFT/RIGHT based on hash + formation.

**Q: What's most important to know?**
A: Where the 3-tech goes and which side that is. Everything else is secondary.

**Q: What about 3-3 Mint fronts?**
A: Completely different - 4i-0-4i-Edge. NO 3T or 1T. Different gap responsibilities.

**Q: Can I see alignment for pressures?**
A: Not yet. DL alignment only shows for Coverage view currently.

**Q: What if alignment says "CRITICAL"?**
A: That means it's mandatory for this situation. Must use that technique.
